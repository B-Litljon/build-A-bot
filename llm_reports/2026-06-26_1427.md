## Honest Verdict on Edge
The strategy **lacks a durable edge under realistic costs**.
A 5-pip spread penalty is a conservative estimate for GBP/JPY during the volatile London open, when spreads tend to blow out. At 5 pips, the OOS Profit Factor is 0.94, yielding a negative expectancy of -1.21 pips per trade.

Furthermore, as Claude pointed out, the 1:2 Risk/Reward is mostly cosmetic; the majority of trades do not hit the stop or target and instead exit at the end of the session at 15:00 UTC. The sliver of zero-cost edge comes from small intraday drift, not from clean breakouts hitting a 2:1 target.

**CRITICAL FLAG:** A worst-case stop of 606 pips would result in a ~$39 loss on that position size—wiping out nearly **10% of the account** in a single trade. This aggressively violates the 1-2% maximum risk tolerance. We cannot safely position-size this strategy on a $200-$400 account without capping the absolute stop-loss, which would further degrade the already negative win-rate/expectancy.

## Strategic Recommendation
Do not proceed with live deployment of this exact iteration. The edge is killed by the spread, and the fat-tail risk (606-pip stops) breaks the $400 account constraints. We must iterate. Options include:
1. Adding a filter (e.g., only trading very tight Asian ranges to improve the R/R math and limit max stop distances).
2. Investigating an earlier time cutoff (e.g., 11:00 UTC) as most genuine momentum might end by mid-London session.
3. Abandoning the 2:1 breakout model entirely in favor of a direct session-drift play.
